## 🌐 What is Accessibility?

Accessibility ensures that digital content can be used by **everyone**, including people with:

- Visual impairments
- Hearing loss
- Mobility limitations
- Cognitive or neurological conditions

Web accessibility means designing and developing websites that are **perceivable**, **operable**, **understandable**, and **robust** for all users.

---

## 🤔 Why Do We Do Accessibility Testing?

### ✅ Legal Compliance

Governments and public sector websites must meet legal accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG 2.1, Equality Act 2010, Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations in the UK).

### 👥 Inclusive Design

Testing ensures that assistive technologies like screen readers, keyboard navigation, or voice control can interact with your content.

### 📈 Better UX for All

Good accessibility often improves overall usability:

- Clear navigation
- Proper contrast
- Readable content

### 🧪 Shift-Left Testing

Adding automated accessibility checks to CI/CD ensures issues are caught early, reducing risk and rework.

---

## 🛠️ How We Test Accessibility

We use:

- **axe-core** — an open-source accessibility testing engine
- **Playwright** — to run end-to-end accessibility checks across pages
- **HTML Reports** — to view, debug, and fix issues efficiently
